## The short version

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your firm's online presence to be cited by AI-powered engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and the AI features increasingly embedded in Google Search itself.

If SEO is about getting ranked, GEO is about getting cited.

## Why this matters for consulting firms

Your prospective clients aren't just Googling anymore. They're asking AI engines questions like:

- "Which firms specialize in supply chain consulting for mid-market manufacturers?"
- "What are the best approaches to digital transformation in financial services?"
- "Who are the leading management consultants in operational efficiency?"

When an AI engine answers these questions, it cites sources. Those sources become the shortlist. If your firm isn't among them, you're not part of the conversation — regardless of how good your Google rankings are.

## How GEO differs from traditional SEO

Traditional SEO and GEO share some DNA, but they optimize for fundamentally different evaluation systems.

**Traditional SEO** optimizes for Google's ranking algorithm. It focuses on keywords, backlinks, page speed, and structured markup to earn a position in search results. The user sees a list of links and chooses which to click.

**GEO** optimizes for how AI models evaluate and select sources. AI engines don't present a list of links — they synthesize information from multiple sources and cite the ones they deem most authoritative and relevant. The evaluation criteria include:

- **Entity clarity** — Does the AI model understand who you are, what you do, and what you're an authority on?
- **Content structure** — Is your content organized in ways that AI models can parse and extract key claims from?
- **Source authority** — Does your firm appear across multiple trusted sources, reinforcing your expertise?
- **Citability** — Does your content contain clear, factual, well-attributed statements that an AI engine would feel confident citing?

## What consulting firms should prioritize

Based on how current AI engines evaluate and cite sources, consulting firms should focus on:

### 1. Entity optimization

Make sure AI engines understand your firm as a distinct entity. This means consistent naming, clear descriptions of what you do, structured data (schema markup), and presence across authoritative sources that AI models train on.

### 2. Structured, authoritative content

Write content that makes clear, specific claims backed by evidence. AI engines favor content that is:

- Factual and specific (not vague or promotional)
- Well-structured with clear headings and logical flow
- Authoritative in tone and sourced where appropriate

### 3. Topical depth over breadth

AI engines assess topical authority. A firm with 20 deep, interconnected articles on supply chain resilience will be cited more than a firm with 200 shallow posts on random business topics. Go deep on what you know.

### 4. Third-party validation

AI models don't just look at your website. They evaluate your presence across the web — industry publications, directories, news mentions, and other sources. Building authority signals across multiple channels reinforces your citability.

## The intersection with SEO

GEO doesn't replace SEO — it builds on it. A strong technical SEO foundation (fast site, proper schema markup, clean architecture) makes your content more accessible to AI crawlers. Strong content authority serves both Google's ranking algorithm and AI citation models.

The firms that will win visibility over the next five years are the ones building for both channels simultaneously — getting ranked in search results AND cited in AI-generated answers. That's what we mean by AI visibility infrastructure.

## What to do next

If you're a consulting firm wondering where you stand on AI visibility, start with an honest assessment:

1. Search for your firm's name in ChatGPT and Perplexity. What do they say about you?
2. Ask AI engines questions in your area of expertise. Are you cited?
3. Evaluate your content — is it structured for citability, or just for keyword rankings?

These three checks will tell you more about your AI visibility gap than any tool or report.
